Muizz Street

Al-Muizz Street is the vibrant historic heart of Islamic Cairo and one of the world's most spectacular open-air museums. Stretching for nearly one kilometer, this pedestrian-friendly thoroughfare contains the largest and densest concentration of medieval architectural treasures in the Islamic world. Walking along its cobblestones reveals an overwhelming display of towering minarets, stunning Fatimid and Mamluk mosques, historic schoolhouses (madrasas), and ornate public water fountains

Fouad Street

Fouad Street (Tareek El Horeya) is Alexandria’s most iconic and historic thoroughfare. Tracing its roots back to 331 BC, it is widely considered the world's oldest continuously inhabited planned street. It is famed for its European-style Belle Époque architecture, fine dining, and vibrant arts scene

Corniche street

The Alexandria Corniche is a scenic 17-kilometer waterfront promenade along the Mediterranean Sea, stretching from Qaitbay Citadel to Montaza Palace. As the city's vibrant heart, it features beautiful beaches, historic architecture, bustling cafes, and iconic landmarks like Stanley Bridge. It is the ultimate spot to enjoy fresh sea breezes and stunning coastal sunsets.
